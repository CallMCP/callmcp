# Who We Are: adversarial review

Date: 2026-07-10

Three independent reviewers attacked `WHO-WE-ARE.md` after it was written:

- skeptical AI automation builder;
- hostile competitor strategist;
- developer-growth operator.

## Consensus

The thesis is strategically right, but the moat is mostly designed rather
than earned.

| Stage | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Acquisition | Strong hook: give an agent phone capability through an open contract. |
| Activation | Weak: the first ten minutes are not yet reliably proven. |
| Retention | Unproven: builders retain around repeated deployment savings, not abstractions. |
| Monetization | Plausible: OSS wedge → KaiCalls managed route, if the handoff stays coherent. |

## Corrections accepted into the document

1. CallMCP OSS, the KaiCalls driver, and the broader KaiCalls hosted API/MCP
   are separate surfaces.
2. “Any provider” is an ecosystem goal; current claims must say certified
   drivers and identify what is live-proven.
3. Approval is an auditable application-authorization primitive, not a blanket
   consent or legal-compliance guarantee.
4. Verified outcomes are a target standard until route-level completion and
   evidence metrics exist.
5. Open source is distribution, not the moat by itself.
6. Virality requires a traveling artifact: a privacy-safe proof receipt that
   shows outcome class, evidence availability, policy checks, and route health.
7. The true activation moment is one approved, inspectable phone task—not merely
   installation or a tool list.

## P0 product/documentation blockers

- Refresh stale `BUILD_STATUS.md` claims before using it as public proof.
- Align hosted examples with the actual `CALLMCP_DRIVER_TYPE` and JSON
  credential configuration path.
- Make `doctor` distinguish “credentials configured” from provider reachability,
  number readiness, webhook/media readiness, and activation readiness.
- Resolve any `end_call` contradiction before claiming a hosted capability.
- Remove or clearly label unsupported x402, consent, highest-performance, and
  lowest-burden claims.

## Tests that decide whether the strategy is real

1. Cold-start test: 10–15 builders, public docs only, 70% meaningful activation
   in ten minutes without human help.
2. Onboarding experiment: OSS-first versus managed-first; compare first-task
   completion and KaiCalls conversion.
3. Retention test: 5–10 builders, one recurring live use case, four weeks,
   measuring repeat tasks, evidence retrieval, failure recovery, and founder
   intervention.

## Bottom line

The defensible message today is:

> CallMCP is an open, provider-neutral interface for phone actions. KaiCalls
> is the managed route for builders who want someone else to operate the
> communications stack.

The stronger “verified outcomes” story should be earned with live certified
routes and published evidence, not declared ahead of the proof.
